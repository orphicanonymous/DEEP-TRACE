 Project Synopsis 
 Title  :  Deep Trace: Forensic Detection of AI-Generated Audio-video Content 
 Problem Statement: 
 The  rapid  evolution  of  generative  artificial  intelligence  has  made  it  increasingly  easy 
 to  create  highly  realistic  synthetic  audio  and  video  content,  commonly  known  as 
 deepfakes.  While  these  technologies  have  beneficial  applications  in  entertainment 
 and  media  production,  they  also  pose  serious  risks  in  the  form  of  misinformation, 
 identity  impersonation,  financial  fraud,  and  manipulation  of  public  opinion.These 
 manipulated  media  forms  pose  serious  threats  to  digital  trust,  cybersecurity, 
 journalism,  legal  systems,  and  public  safety  by  enabling  misinformation,  identity 
 theft, and fraudulent activities. 
 Objectives: 
 ●  To  develop  a  web-based  deepfake  detection  system  with  automated 
 preprocessing  and  multimodal  analysis  of  spatial,  temporal,  and  audio 
 features. 
 ●  To  implement  an  advanced  forensic  module  combining  rPPG-based  biological 
 signal  extraction  and  cross-modal  consistency  analysis  for  robust  and 
 explainable audio–video deepfake detection. 
 ●  To  generate  probabilistic  authenticity  confidence  scores,  enabling  accountable 
 and evidence-based validation of media integrity for reliable decision-making.

 Proposed Methodology: 
 Fig.  1  presents  the  DeepTrace  multimodal  forensic  system  designed  to  authenticate 
 AI-generated  audio–video  content.  The  input  media  stream  is  processed  through 
 parallel  visual  and  audio  pipelines,  where  video  frames  undergo  spatial  and  temporal 
 feature  extraction  while  the  audio  track  is  analyzed  for  spectral  and  speech-based 
 irregularities.  A  cross-modal  transformer  evaluates  lip–speech  synchronization  and 
 motion–audio  alignment,  supported  by  an  rPPG  module  that  derives  heartbeat 
 signals  from  facial  color  variations  to  assess  biological  authenticity.  These  cues  are 
 combined  within  a  unified  forensic  engine,  which  generates  a  probabilistic 
 authenticity  score  along  with  interpretable  evidence  such  as  heatmaps  and  anomaly 
 timestamps, delivering a transparent and reliable deepfake detection output. 
 Technology Stack: 
 Fig.  2  illustrates  the  complete  technology  stack  of  the  DeepTrace  system,  structured 
 across  frontend,  backend,  deep  learning,  datasets,  and  database  layers.  The 
 frontend  interface  is  developed  using  HTML,  CSS,  JavaScript,  and  React.js  to 
 provide  an  interactive  and  user-friendly  media  upload  environment.  The  backend  is 
 implemented  in  Python  using  Flask/FastAPI  to  handle  request  processing, 
 automated preprocessing, and AI model inference.
